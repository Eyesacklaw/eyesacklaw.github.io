About Me

Ok, time for a long introduction...

I'm Isaac, and I'm from Hong Kong. Currently, I am studying abroad at LSE, which is approximately 9616.28 kilometers away from my home.

As you can already tell, I like analysing numbers - that's probably the reason why I chose to study finance in the first place. I like looking at macroeconomic data, such as consumer and producer price indicies, non-farm payrolls, and even implicit statistics such as Fed Fund Futures, to understand our economy and predict what may happen in markets. I'm also curious as to what drives the growth of a company, and its share price, prompting me to read their annual reports and investigate their competitive advantages. Ultimately, these factors would help me develop a prediction of what would happen a stock, and it brings me great satisfaction to see my trade ideas be executed in real life.

But what is finance without coding? I like problem solving, applying algorithms to real data, and analysing them with graphs. Even if that means pulling my hair out when I'm debugging code. Being proficient in Python with 4 years of experience, I've been experimenting and using it for data analysis. I've also been applying more complex mathematical models into my code, and although it takes a long and painful time to understand, it brings me great joy when I finally get it.

I also like learning new things: practicing for the CFA, taking German classes at university, cooking meals, and exploring different societies. Beyond academics, I write frequently, whether that is self-publishing my book on Amazon, or contributing to my school's blogs / magazines. I think writing (literature) is the best way to express oneself, in the sense that complex ideas and emotions can be conveyed eloquently and simplistically. Music too - I really love classical music. Rachmaninoff's Piano Concerto No. 2 and Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1 are two of my favourites.

That's basically me summarised in a few paragraphs.
